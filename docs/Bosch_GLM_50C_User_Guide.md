# Bosch GLM 50 C Professional — Comprehensive User Guide

A detailed, step-by-step guide to operating the Bosch GLM 50 C laser distance measurer, covering setup, every measurement function, Bluetooth pairing with the MeasureOn app, basic settings, accuracy checks, troubleshooting, and care. Written for first-time users and as a reference for professionals.

---

## 1. About the Tool

The Bosch GLM 50 C Professional is a Class 2 laser distance measurer with a 635 nm laser diode and a 0.05 m to 50 m (about 2 in to 165 ft) measuring range. Typical accuracy is ±1.5 mm. It is dust and splash protected to IP54, drop-tested to 1 m, and ships in a soft pouch with two AAA batteries.

It is positioned by Bosch for architects, construction managers, electricians, drywall installers, carpenters, and painters who need to capture floor areas, wall areas, and room volumes. Key differentiators versus simpler laser measures are the rotating colour display, the 360° tilt sensor (inclinometer), the stake-out function, and Bluetooth transfer to the Bosch MeasureOn app.

## 4. Safety First (Read Before Use)

The GLM 50 C uses a Class 2 visible red laser. It is safe to use if a few rules are followed:

- Never point the laser at people, animals, vehicles, or reflective surfaces aimed back at people.
- Never look directly into the beam, including its reflection. The blink reflex is the only thing protecting your eye; do not override it with laser-viewing glasses or optical instruments such as binoculars or telescopes.
- Do not operate the tool in explosive atmospheres or near flammable liquids, gases, or dust.
- Do not modify the device, open the housing, or remove warning labels.
- Keep the device away from children.
- Avoid carrying it pressed against the body for long periods.
- Only use 2 × AAA alkaline (LR03) batteries; remove batteries if storing for long periods to prevent leakage.
- Operating temperature is roughly −10 °C to +45 °C; storage range is wider but extreme heat or cold will degrade accuracy.

## 5. Inserting the Batteries

1. Turn the tool over so the rear of the housing is facing you.
2. Press the latch on the battery compartment cover (part 11) and slide it open.
3. Insert two 1.5 V AAA (LR03) batteries, aligning the plus and minus terminals exactly as shown inside the compartment.
4. Replace the cover and snap it shut.
5. If the battery icon on the display blinks or shows empty, replace both batteries together; never mix used and new cells.

Use fresh, high-quality alkaline batteries. Rechargeable NiMH cells will work but give shorter runtime and a lower battery-icon reading.

## 6. Powering On and Off, and First Look at the Display

**Power on:** Briefly press the red measuring button (2) or the On/Off button (5). The display lights up and the laser is armed but not yet emitted.

**Power off:** Press and hold the On/Off button (5) for about three seconds. The tool also powers itself off after five minutes without a key press.

**Display elements you will see**

- **a** Bluetooth status (connected/active)
- **b** Battery indicator
- **c** Inclination value (degrees) when the tilt sensor is in use
- **d** Function icon (length, area, volume, indirect, etc.)
- **e** Result line (bottom of display) — the most recent measurement
- **f** Intermediate values — earlier measurements in a multi-step function appear above the result
- **g** Reference symbol — shows whether the tool is measuring from the rear edge, front edge, or tripod thread
- **h** Status bar — Bluetooth, battery, laser-active icons
- **i** Basic Settings indicator when that menu is open

The display rotates automatically into portrait or landscape orientation thanks to the tilt sensor.

## 7. Setting the Measuring Reference Point

The tool can measure from three different reference points. Choosing the correct one removes a common source of error.

1. Press the reference button (8) repeatedly to cycle through the options.
2. Watch the symbol (g) on the display:
   - **Rear edge** (default) — measurement starts from the back of the housing. Use when you are pressing the back of the tool flat against a wall.
   - **Front edge** — measurement starts from the front face of the tool. Use when you butt the front of the tool against the starting surface (for example, in a narrow space).
   - **Tripod thread** — measurement starts from the centre of the 1/4 in thread on the base. Use when the tool is mounted on a tripod.
3. The chosen reference remains active until you change it, even after the tool is powered off, so confirm it before each new job.

A wrong reference is the single most common cause of measurements being off by the length of the housing (about 100 mm).

## 8. Taking a Basic Length Measurement

By default, the GLM 50 C powers up in real-time / single-length mode.

1. Press the measuring button (2) once. The red laser dot appears on the target.
2. Aim the laser at the point you want to measure to. Hold the tool steady; for distances over 10 m use both hands or rest it on a flat surface.
3. Press the measuring button (2) again. After a fraction of a second a beep sounds (if beeps are enabled) and the distance is shown on the result line.
4. To take another measurement, press (2) again; the previous reading moves up into the intermediate-values area.
5. To clear the last reading without affecting stored memory, press the On/Off button (5) briefly.

**Tips for clean readings**

- The laser will not return useful data from a polished mirror or highly absorptive (matte black) target. Place the included target card on the surface if needed.
- In bright outdoor light the dot can be hard to see; use the target card and, if possible, shade the receiving lens (part 14).
- If you get an "Error" message, switch the tool off and back on and re-take the measurement.

## 9. Continuous (Real-Time) Measurement

Continuous measurement updates the distance about twice per second, so you can move toward or away from a wall and watch the value change in real time. It also tracks minimum and maximum distances during the sweep.

1. Press [Func] (7) to open the function menu.
2. Use [+] (3) or [−] (4) to select the continuous-measurement icon (it looks like a laser beam with sweeping arrows).
3. Confirm with [Func] (7).
4. Briefly press the measuring button (2) to switch the laser on.
5. Move the tool toward or away from the target. The current distance is shown on the result line; the minimum and maximum reached so far are shown above it.
6. Press the measuring button (2) again to freeze the current value. The minimum and maximum remain visible.
7. To exit, press [Func] (7) and choose another function.

Use this mode for diagonal sweeps in a room (the minimum distance to a wall is the perpendicular distance), or for laying out a length by walking out from a wall until the desired distance is reached.

## 10. Area Measurement

1. Press [Func] (7), select the area icon (a rectangle with two double-headed arrows), and confirm with [Func] (7).
2. Press the measuring button (2) to take the first length (for example, the long side of the room).
3. Reposition and press (2) to take the second length (for example, the short side).
4. The area is calculated automatically and shown on the result line. The two source measurements remain visible above it.

The tool always uses the last two distance measurements taken in this mode; if either of them is wrong, press (5) to clear it and re-measure.

---

## 11. Volume Measurement

1. Press [Func] (7), select the volume icon (a cube), and confirm.
2. Measure the length by pressing (2).
3. Measure the width by pressing (2) again.
4. Measure the height by pressing (2) a third time.
5. The volume is calculated and displayed on the result line, with all three source measurements visible above it.

Volume is useful for calculating room air volume for HVAC sizing, concrete pours, container capacity, and so on.

## 12. Wall Area Measurement

This mode is built for painters and drywall installers: it sums multiple wall lengths that share a common ceiling height to give a total paintable area.

1. Press [Func] (7), select the wall-area icon (several rectangles sharing one height), and confirm.
2. Measure the **ceiling height H** first by pressing (2). This single height will be applied to every subsequent wall length.
3. Measure the **length of the first wall L₁** by pressing (2). The first sub-area H × L₁ is calculated and shown.
4. Press [+] (3) to add another wall.
5. Measure the length of the next wall L₂. The new sub-area H × L₂ is added to the running total.
6. Repeat for as many walls as you have. The running total is always visible on the result line.
7. To exclude an opening (such as a window or door), press [−] (4) before measuring its length, and that sub-area will be subtracted from the total.

This is far faster than measuring each wall, then each opening, then adding and subtracting on a calculator.

## 13. Indirect Measurement Using the Pythagorean Theorem

Indirect measurement lets you find a distance you cannot aim the laser along directly, by measuring related distances and using the built-in tilt sensor or pure Pythagoras. There are three variants on the GLM 50 C.

### 13.1 Single indirect height (one measurement plus tilt)

Use this when you can see the top of an object (such as a tree, a lamp post, or a high ceiling) but not its base, and you can stand at a known reference level (such as the ground at your feet).

1. Press [Func] (7), select the single-indirect-height icon, and confirm.
2. Aim the laser at the top of the object and press (2). The tool records the slant distance and the inclination angle from the tilt sensor.
3. The vertical height H is calculated as `H = distance × sin(angle)` and shown on the result line.

The angle is taken automatically; you do not have to enter it. Keep the tool as steady as possible during the measurement, because a small angle error compounds over distance.

### 13.2 Single indirect length (two measurements, pure Pythagoras)

Use this when there is an obstacle (such as a piece of furniture) between you and the wall, and you need the horizontal length AB along the wall.

1. Press [Func] (7), select the single-indirect-length icon, and confirm.
2. Place the tool on the starting point A of the required distance.
3. Tilt the tool so that the laser is aligned with end point B (the laser strikes a point on the wall level with B) and press (2). This is **measurement 1**.
4. Hold the tool steady and turn it so the laser is perpendicular to the same wall (parallel to the desired AB). Press (2). This is **measurement 2**.
5. The required distance AB is calculated as `AB = sqrt(measurement1² − measurement2²)` and shown on the result line, with both source measurements above it.

The key constraint is that the second measurement must be perpendicular to the wall; otherwise the geometry breaks and the result is wrong.

### 13.3 Combined indirect height (three measurements)

Use this when you need a vertical distance between two inaccessible points, such as the height of a window opening that is high on a wall.

1. Press [Func] (7), select the combined-indirect-height icon, and confirm.
2. Measure the slant distance to the top of the object and press (2).
3. Measure perpendicularly to the wall and press (2).
4. Measure the slant distance to the bottom of the object and press (2).
5. The vertical distance between the two points is calculated and shown.

In all three indirect modes, the tool's tilt sensor is doing real work. Steady the device against a fixed object if possible; results are sensitive to angular wobble.

## 14. Stake-Out (Marking Equal Lengths)

Stake-out is used to mark a series of equally spaced points, such as for stud or joist layout, tile spacing, or fence posts.

1. Press [Func] (7), select the stake-out icon, and confirm.
2. Use [+] (3) and [−] (4) to set the desired step length (minimum 0.1 m, maximum 50 m). Hold the buttons to scroll quickly.
3. Press the measuring button (2). The laser comes on and continuously measures the distance from the tool to the wall or starting reference.
4. Slowly move the tool away from the starting point. The display shows the defined length and the current distance to the next marker. Arrows at the top or bottom of the screen indicate how far you are from the next stake-out point.
5. Mark the surface when the arrows are centred and the distance matches the set length.
6. Continue moving in the same direction and the tool will count out the next marker, and so on.
7. To change the step length, press [Func] (7) and re-enter the value.

Note from Bosch: in stake-out mode the displayed distance is from the start point to the marker, not from the rear edge of the tool, regardless of the reference setting.

## 15. Digital Level (Tilt / Inclinometer)

The GLM 50 C has a 360° tilt sensor that can be used as a digital spirit level independently of distance measurement.

1. Press [Func] (7) repeatedly (or scroll with [+] / [−]) until the bubble-level icon is selected, then confirm with [Func] (7).
2. The display shows the inclination of the tool's long edge in degrees, plus a graphical bubble.
3. Place the tool on the surface to be levelled and rotate or adjust until the angle reads 0.00° (for level) or 90.00° (for plumb).
4. The display flips between landscape and portrait so the reading stays the right way up.

The level is also useful as a check when using the indirect-measurement functions, since those rely on the same tilt sensor.

## 16. Adding and Subtracting Measurements

You can build up running totals or differences in any single-length measurement mode.

- After a measurement is on the result line, press [+] (3). A plus sign appears.
- Take another measurement; the new value is added to the previous one and the sum is shown.
- Press [−] (4) instead of [+] to subtract.
- Press [+] or [−] again to chain further operations.
- Press the On/Off button (5) briefly to cancel the operation.

This is how you total several wall sections, or subtract a window opening from a wall length.

## 17. Memory: Recalling and Deleting Past Measurements

The GLM 50 C automatically stores the **last 30 measurements** of any type (length, area, volume, indirect, etc.). They survive a power cycle.

**To recall a stored value**

1. Press [Func] (7) to open the function menu.
2. Use [+] (3) or [−] (4) to scroll to the memory icon and confirm with [Func] (7).
3. The most recent stored measurement appears. Use [+] and [−] to scroll forward and backward through the list.

**To delete a single stored value**

1. Recall the value as above.
2. With it on the display, press the On/Off button (5) briefly. That entry is removed.

**To delete the entire memory**

1. Open the memory list as above.
2. Press and hold the On/Off button (5) for a few seconds. The list is cleared.

A confirmation prompt may appear before the clear is executed; press [Func] (7) to confirm or (5) to cancel.

## 18. Bluetooth and the Bosch MeasureOn App

The "C" in GLM 50 C indicates the integrated Bluetooth interface. It pairs with the free Bosch MeasureOn app on iOS and Android, which lets you build floor plans, attach photos to measurements, sketch and annotate, and export to PDF, JPG, or other formats.

### 18.1 Installing the app

1. On your phone, open the App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android).
2. Search for **Bosch MeasureOn**.
3. Install and open it. Allow Bluetooth permissions.

### 18.2 Pairing the GLM 50 C

1. On the tool, press the Bluetooth button (6). The Bluetooth icon appears in the status bar and the tool starts advertising.
2. In MeasureOn, tap the connect / device icon and choose **Add device** or **Connect laser measure**.
3. The app lists nearby Bosch tools. Select the one matching the serial number on the back of your GLM 50 C (part 13).
4. Once connected, the Bluetooth icon (a) on the tool shows the active connection.
5. Take a measurement on the tool. The value is transferred to the app immediately when you confirm with the measuring button (2), and is placed at the current cursor position in the active drawing or note.

### 18.3 Deactivating Bluetooth

- Press the Bluetooth button (6) once to open the Bluetooth menu.
- Press the Bluetooth button (6) again to switch the radio off. The icon disappears.
- Bluetooth also switches off when the tool powers down.

Tip: when on a long site session, leaving Bluetooth off saves battery; turn it on only when you are actively transferring values.

## 20. Accuracy Check and Care

Bosch recommends regular accuracy checks for both distance and inclination, especially after a fall, a long storage period, or large temperature changes.

### 20.1 Distance accuracy check

1. Choose a fixed, well-defined distance between 1 m and 10 m whose length you know exactly (a piece of tape measure stretched along a wall works).
2. Set the reference point appropriately (front edge if you butt the tool against the start point).
3. Take ten measurements of the same distance.
4. The spread between any two readings should be within ±2 mm. A deviation of more than ±1.5 mm per measurement, or systematic drift, means the device should be recalibrated by an authorised Bosch service centre.

### 20.2 Inclination accuracy check

1. Place the tool on a flat surface that you have already verified is horizontal with a known reliable level.
2. Read the angle; it should be 0.00° within ±0.2°.
3. Rotate the tool 180° on the same surface and read again. The two readings should be equal in magnitude; a difference indicates the tilt sensor needs recalibration.

### 20.3 Cleaning and storage

- Wipe the housing with a soft, slightly damp cloth. Do not use solvents or abrasives.
- Keep the reception lens (14) and laser outlet (15) clean; smudges directly reduce range and accuracy. Use a microfibre cloth.
- Do not submerge the tool. The IP54 rating handles splashes, not immersion.
- Remove the batteries if storing for more than a few weeks to prevent leakage damage.
- Store in the supplied pouch at room temperature.

## 21. Error Messages and Troubleshooting

The tool monitors its own operation and shows an error message on the display when a measurement fails or a fault is detected. The two most common are:

- **Error** — a measurement could not be completed correctly (poor reflection, motion, out of range, or operating temperature out of spec). Switch the tool off and on and repeat the measurement.
- **Temperature warning (thermometer icon)** — the tool is too hot or too cold. Move into the −10 °C to +45 °C operating range and let it stabilise before measuring.

### Common issues and fixes

| Symptom | Likely cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Laser dot invisible outdoors | Bright sunlight | Use the target card, shade the target, or take the measurement at dawn/dusk |
| Display shows wildly wrong distance | Wrong reference point set | Press the reference button (8) and check the symbol (g) before measuring |
| Repeated Error in long measurements | Reflective or dark target, or beam obstructed | Place a target card on the surface; check the reception lens is clean |
| Tool will not pair with MeasureOn | Bluetooth off on phone, or already paired to another phone | Toggle Bluetooth on both sides; in the phone settings, "forget" the device and re-pair |
| Battery icon empty almost immediately | Mixed old and new cells, or weak batteries | Replace both AAA cells together with fresh alkalines |
| Inclination reads off when laid flat | Sensor needs calibration, or surface is not actually level | Run the accuracy check in section 20.2 |
| Tool powers off after 5 minutes mid-job | Standard auto-off | Press any button or take a measurement to keep it awake; no setting disables this |
| Display flips at the wrong moment | Rotating-display setting on | Disable rotation in Basic Settings if you prefer a fixed orientation |

If error messages persist after a clean restart and a battery swap, contact Bosch service. Do not open the housing.

## 22. Practical Workflow Examples

A few short walkthroughs to put the functions together.

### 22.1 Measuring a room for flooring

1. Set the reference to **rear edge** and press the back of the tool flat against one of the long walls.
2. Switch to **area** mode (section 10).
3. Measure the long side, then the short side. Read the area off the result line.
4. To total several rooms, switch back to single-length mode, then use the [+] button between area results to build a running total of square metres.

### 22.2 Estimating paint for a four-walled room with a door and window

1. Switch to **wall area** mode (section 12).
2. Measure the ceiling height H once.
3. Measure each of the four wall lengths L, pressing [+] before each new length.
4. Before measuring the door width and the window width, press [−] so those sub-areas are subtracted from the total.
5. The result on the screen is the net paintable area.

### 22.3 Finding the height of a tall lamp post you cannot reach

1. Stand at the base of the post on level ground and switch to **single indirect height** (section 13.1).
2. Tilt the tool to aim at the top of the post and press the measuring button.
3. The result line shows the vertical height; the slant distance and angle are above it.

### 22.4 Laying out floor joists every 600 mm

1. Switch to **stake-out** (section 14).
2. Set the step length to 0.600 m using [+] and [−].
3. Press the back of the tool against the starting wall and start the measurement.
4. Slide the tool along the rim joist; mark each point where the arrows centre and the distance reads 0.600 m, 1.200 m, 1.800 m, and so on.
